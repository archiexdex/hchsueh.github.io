About me

I am Hsea-Ching Hsueh 薛世卿, a Taiwanese college graduate. I received my Bachelor of Science degree in Electrical Engineering at National Taiwan University in 2015. I have been working with Professor Shao-Yi Chien on self-adaptive processor design for real-time continuous time-frequency analysis tailored to wearable biomedical application, which led to multiple conference publications. I also worked with Professor Polly Huang on prototyping smart wear-and-tear device for fitness tracking. I am currently in the one-year compulsory military service of Taiwan.

My research interest lies in energy-efficient digital and mixed-signal integrated circuit and system design for biomedical and biotechnological applications. Being an engineer with awareness to societal issues, I want to devote my energy and skills to tackling the problems in aging population and growing wealth gap. My career goal is to develop biomedical electronics to democratize the majority’s access to medical services and thus help transform the current inefficient and costly healthcare system.

I have passionate interests beyond my engineering background. Musical art and culinary art take up a large portion of my life. I also enjoy traveling, swimming, and playing badminton.
